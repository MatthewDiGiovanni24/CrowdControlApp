CSC 4700 Project by Matthew DiGiovanni, Connor Smith, and Joseph Griffin

Crowd Control is a mobile app where users can take a picture of public facilities/locations around campus, and the app would determine how busy the location is based on the picture. This would help people decide if/when they want to go to a dining hall, the gym, or a park based on how busy it currently is. The data would be mostly filled with user-submitted data, but could work with built-in cameras as well. The technology would use simple computer vision on pictures to determine the number of people. The data would also be recorded, and users could look at how busy places are over time and look for patterns.
